Light Field Microscopy

3D imaging with Light Field Microscopy and Super-Resolution WeeklyReport


Light Field describes both the 2D location and 2D angle of the incident light, which allows the synthesis of a focal stack computationally, and also enables 3D restruction from one single sensor image. This gives rise to superb temporal resolution in 3D functional biological imaging.

One way to capture the light field is to insert a microlens array in the intermediate plane of the microscope and it will re-distribute the pixels behind to record both the angular and lateral information. And for this reason, what it does is actually to sacrifice the lateral resolution for extra volume depth. In the left picture, the gray channel is reconstructed from Light Field while the green channel is captured under widefield. I tried to build the super-resolution model through machine learning by matching the LR Light Field volume with HR volume acquired by scanning method. This project is in process and the link above is from a certain weekly report, which shows what a worm look like under LFM.

Other Projects and Works

Shearing Interference
Beam Profile Assessment Based on Lateral Shearing Interference PDF(CN)


With lateral shearing interference pattern we can infer Lateral Shearing Interference is a reference-free technique. The wavefront under test interferes with a laterally sheared copy of itself in the superposed range. The information of the wavefront, as well as the beam profile, can be inferred by the interference pattern.

The project aimed to take advantage of this technique to undertake fast measurement of beam's colllimation, M^2, Rayleigh distance, wavefront, providing a possible solution for portable beam profiler. We designed a so-called Dual Shearing Interference System, in which we imitate Michelson Interferometer so that the laser beam could intefere twice in one go. We took Raspberry Pi as platform and made use of OpenCV to recognize the inteference pattern and inter the beam's parameters.

Electronic Design and Micromouse Team
Maze Solver Robot video


We developed a tiny robot car to automatically solve unknow maze. The robot was running RTOS on TivaC microcontroller and perceives the environment via infrared light. The main algorithm, Flood Fill, is to imitate the water flowing! The water would go through open track and be blocked when met with dead ending. Check the video above to see what the robot does!
