BananaPi

Take a Look

This morning I get my BananaPi M3. I bought it for a consideration that it may replace RaspberryPi 3B in our application in embedded digital image processing. Quate from offical website:
> Banana Pi M3 is a super charged single board computer with an Octa-core processor and 2GB of RAM. Along side the elite processing unit, it features Gigabit Ethernet, 2 USB, SATA, WiFi, Bluetooth, and HDMI connection. It can run on a variety of operating systems including Android, Lubuntu, Ubuntu, Debian, and Raspbian.



Open it:)! We have our BananaPi. We can notice the processor A83T, which is based on eight ARM Cortex-A7 CPU core operating at hight frequency up to 2.0GHz.

Oops. Different CSI ports


Compared to Raspberry Pi 3B+, BPI M3 has actually different CSI(camera interface) pin set. While RPI has 15 pins as serial port for camera data exchange, BPI has up to 40 pins for 8 parallel data channels. According to its gitbooks. That’s too bad. I has thought about taking advantage of the built in ISP image signal processor, which can support 8MP camera interface and I might use RPI camera directly. Also, the commonds like raspistill and modules like ‘picamera’ seem unable to function, even if we install Raspbian System on BPI. :(
Luckily, community has made contribution to provide camera suitable for BPI.–>Lemaker’s camera Hope we can find a lot information on the BPI’s forum.
